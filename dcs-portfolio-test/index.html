HBR Articles

While studying leaders and organizations that attract, develop, and manage talent so as to foster engagement and creativity, I found many insights in the pages of HBR.

How Pixar Fosters Collective Creativity

by Ed Catmull (September 2008).
Pixar Animation Studios builds a rebel workforce from day one: During onboarding sessions, it highlights past company mistakes so that new hires will know it’s OK to fail.

Are You a High Potential?

by Douglas A. Ready, Jay A. Conger, and Linda A. Hill (June 2010)

How to Hang On to Your High Potentials

by Claudio Fernández-Aráoz, Boris Groysberg, and Nitin Nohria (October 2011).
To help fight conformity, companies can assess and develop employees’ curiosity—a good indicator of potential and useful trait for noncomformity.

How GE Teaches Teams to Lead Change

by Steven Prokesch (January 2009)
HBR senior editor Steven Prokesch, who worked w/ith me on “Rebel Talent,” put together this excellent feature on how to use language to disrupt the status quo.

Managing Without Managers

by Ricardo Semler (September–October 1989)

Why My Former Employees Still Work for Me

by Ricardo Semler (January–February 1994)
The author’s company, Semco, is a classic case of an organization that was structured to encourage nonconformity. These articles will help others follow suit.
